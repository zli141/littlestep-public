LittleStep — Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 11, 2026

LittleStep is a private baby journal for families. We designed it to keep your family's photos and records private. This policy explains what we collect and how it is used.

What you store, and where

The content you create — photos, videos, comments, reactions, growth records, feeding logs, and solid-food records — is stored in Apple iCloud (CloudKit) under your own iCloud account. It is visible only to the family members you explicitly invite to a baby's family circle. We do not run our own servers that store this content, and we cannot read it.

One exception: to show family visit statistics, the app stores a minimal visit counter — your in-app nickname, visit count, and last visit time, never photos or content — in the app's shared CloudKit database rather than your personal iCloud storage.

Your account

LittleStep identifies you through your device's iCloud account. Due to Apple's privacy rules, the app cannot see your Apple ID email or name. The nickname and avatar shown in the app are set by you within the app.

Advertising

For non-subscribers, LittleStep shows banner ads via Google AdMob. AdMob may collect device identifiers and similar data to serve and measure ads. On first launch we ask for your permission (App Tracking Transparency); you can decline, and you can change this anytime in iOS Settings → Privacy & Security → Tracking. We request only general-audience (G-rated) ad content. See Google's policy: policies.google.com/technologies/ads.

Ad-free membership

You can purchase an auto-renewing subscription to remove all ads. Payments are handled by Apple; we do not receive your payment details. You can manage or cancel anytime in iOS Settings → Apple ID → Subscriptions.

What we do NOT do

  • We do not sell your data.
  • We do not share your photos, videos, comments, or records with any third party (they stay in your iCloud, shared only with family you invite).
  • We do not use your content for advertising.

Children

LittleStep is intended for parents and family members (adults). It is not directed to children under 13, and ads are configured as non-child-directed, general-audience.

Your choices

  • Delete a baby in the app to permanently remove its associated content.
  • Uninstalling the app removes locally cached data; iCloud data follows Apple's iCloud controls.
  • Subscribe to “Ad-Free Membership” to remove all ads.

Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time. Changes will be posted on this page with an updated date above.
